About

Service & Scholar

Service & Scholar is a project built to help active duty service members earn degrees while still in uniform without burning out, losing momentum, or wasting their education benefits.

Mission

To provide clear, practical guidance that helps service members use their education benefits wisely and finish degrees that support their long-term goals in and out of the military.

The focus is on being realistic and respectful of the military lifestyle. Long shifts, rotating schedules, and sudden changes are expected here, not treated as excuses. Service & Scholar aims to make college a structured, manageable part of that reality, not something you’re always “meaning to start.”

What you can expect here

  • Step-by-step articles that make complex topics feel simple and actionable.
  • Study and life systems designed around the constraints of active duty schedules.
  • Honest stories and examples, not unrealistic “perfect student” narratives.
  • Resources you can download, adapt, and use immediately.

Who’s behind it

Service & Scholar is run by an active duty service member with over 10 years of continuous service and a parallel track of earning college credits and degrees using military education benefits.

This is an independent educational resource built from lived experience. Service & Scholar is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or an official representative of the U.S. Department of Defense, the Department of Veterans Affairs, or any branch of the U.S. military.

Over that time, I’ve navigated Tuition Assistance, the GI Bill, CLEP and DSST exams, credit transfers, shifting degree plans, and the realities of going to school while working in demanding, dynamic environments.

I’ve made mistakes, taken the long way around on some decisions, and learned how to build systems that actually survive the day-to-day, deployments, and last-minute taskers. Service & Scholar exists so you don’t have to learn all of that the hard way.

Policies and eligibility can vary by branch, command, and time—so the goal here is clarity and direction, with the expectation that you’ll verify final details with your Education Office/ESO, chain of command, and official resources.
